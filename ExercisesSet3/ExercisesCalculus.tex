	\section{Fundamental Theorem of Calculus}
	Let \( f(x) \) be a continuous function on the closed interval \([a, b]\), and let \( F(x) \) be an anti-derivative of \( f(x) \).
	Prove that
	\[
	\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a).
	\]
